Zsigmond Móricz

"Ez ám a muri"

Zsigmond Móricz (1879-1942) was one of the most significant and influential writers in 20th-century Hungarian literature. His work focused on the realistic depiction of the lives of ordinary people and societal injustices. His most famous works, such as "Be Faithful Unto Death" and "Relatives," delve into the depths of human emotions and behavior while also highlighting contemporary social issues. Móricz had a substantial impact on the development of Hungarian prose, and his writings remain relevant to this day. His body of work is an integral part of the Hungarian literary canon and has greatly influenced later generations of writers.

Summary

  • Name: Zsigmond Móricz
  • Lifespan: 1879-1942
  • Occupation: Hungarian writer, editor, and journalist
  • Major Works: Known for novels like "Légy jó mindhalálig" (Be Faithful Unto Death) and "Rokonok" (Relatives), as well as several short stories that depict rural life in Hungary.
  • Writing Style: Emphasized realism and focused on the lives of ordinary people, often addressing societal issues and injustices.
  • Themes: Explored themes of poverty, family dynamics, and social inequality in early 20th-century Hungary.
  • Influence: Móricz had a significant impact on the development of Hungarian literature and played a key role in the Nyugat literary movement.
  • Legacy: His works remain influential in Hungarian literature and are widely studied in schools and universities.

Timeline of his life

  • 1879 - Zsigmond Móricz was born on July 2, 1879, in Tiszacsécse, Hungary.
  • 1902 - Móricz's first short story, "Hét krajcár" ("Seven Kreutzers"), was published, marking the beginning of his literary career.
  • 1925 - Publication of his well-known novel "Légy jó mindhalálig" ("Be Faithful Unto Death").
  • 1932 - Publication of his novel "Rokonok" ("Relatives").
  • 1942 - Zsigmond Móricz passed away on September 5, 1942, in Budapest, Hungary.
