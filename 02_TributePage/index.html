Zsigmond Móricz

"Ez ám a muri"

Zsigmond Móricz (1879-1942) was one of the most significant and influential writers in 20th-century Hungarian literature. His work focused on the realistic depiction of the lives of ordinary people and societal injustices. His most famous works, such as "Be Faithful Unto Death" and "Relatives," delve into the depths of human emotions and behavior while also highlighting contemporary social issues. Móricz had a substantial impact on the development of Hungarian prose, and his writings remain relevant to this day. His body of work is an integral part of the Hungarian literary canon and has greatly influenced later generations of writers.
